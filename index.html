EfficientDreamer: High-Fidelity and Robust 3D Creation via Orthogonal-view Diffusion Prior

Anonymous Authors


Abstract

We introduce DialogFace, a novel method for text-driven facial customization of game characters. Existing text-driven customization systems enable automated character generation but are limited by their one-time generation capability and lack support for iterative editing and fine-tuning, which users require to make further adjustments based on current results to better meet their personalized needs. In this paper, we present an efficient framework that translates multi-round dialogues into continuous facial editing for game characters. Our approach starts by designing a pipeline to generate a game facial editing dataset. Each entry includes an editing prompt, an original facial image, and an edited facial image. We then label the dataset based on human preferences, categorizing samples into winning and losing. Using direct preference optimization, we train a game facial editing diffusion model, called EditPix2Pix-DPO. This model adeptly modifies the original facial image according to the editing prompt, producing edited images that align precisely with semantic descriptions and human preferences. Additionally, this diffusion model can be seamlessly integrated into role-playing game systems, thereby facilitating interactive facial editing of characters. Extensive experimental results demonstrate the efficacy of our proposed method in creating in-game characters and highlight its superior editing performance, particularly in scenarios requiring interactive and iterative facial customization.
